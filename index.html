The History of LEGO

LEGO's history from the start of the company to modern LEGO

1932-1958

LEGO began in 1932 as a workshop run by Kirk Kirtiansen, a carpenter who would make wooden toys like cars and planes. With LEGO's name coming form the two Danish words "Leg Godt" meaning "Play Well".

In 1949 Kirk Kristiansen orders his firdt plastic injection moulding machine and begins making plastic bricks called, "Automatic Binding Bricks,"which were inpisred by Britsih "Kiddi-craft" bricks.

1953 "Automatic Binding Bricks", are renamed as LEGO and two years later in 1955 sets begin to be sold with the idea that the more you have the more you can build.

In 1958 LEGO patents a new coupling principle, thus creating the LEGO brick that we know today.

In 1979 Kjeld Kirk Kristiansen becomes CEO of the LEGO Group. As the first within the family to hol a formal business degree, he brings new ideas into the company.
